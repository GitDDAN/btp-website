Martin Luther

1483 - 1546

German priest, theologian, and key figure in the Protestant Reformation. His challenge to the Roman Catholic Church transformed Christianity and Western civilization.

About Martin Luther

Martin Luther was born in Eisleben, Germany in 1483. An Augustinian monk and professor of theology, Luther's posting of his 95 Theses in 1517 is widely considered the catalyst for the Protestant Reformation.

Luther's writings emphasized salvation by faith alone (sola fide), the authority of Scripture alone (sola scriptura), and the priesthood of all believers. His translation of the Bible into German made Scripture accessible to ordinary people and had a profound impact on both the church and German language.

His courageous stand at the Diet of Worms in 1521, declaring "Here I stand, I can do no other," remains one of the defining moments of church history.

"Unless I am convinced by the testimony of the Scriptures or by clear reason... I am bound by the Scriptures I have quoted and my conscience is captive to the Word of God."

Key Reformation Doctrines

The Five Solas

Sola Scriptura

Scripture alone is the final authority for faith and practice

Sola Fide

Justification is by faith alone, not by works

Sola Gratia

Salvation is by grace alone, a gift from God

Solus Christus

Christ alone is the mediator between God and man

Soli Deo Gloria

All glory belongs to God alone
