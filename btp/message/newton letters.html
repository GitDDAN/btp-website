About John Newton

John Newton (1725-1807) was an English clergyman and author, best known for writing the hymn "Amazing Grace." A former slave trader who experienced a dramatic conversion, Newton became an influential evangelical minister and a leading figure in the abolition movement.

His letters provide profound spiritual wisdom, pastoral counsel, and encouragement drawn from his own remarkable journey from darkness to light. These writings continue to inspire and strengthen believers in their Christian walk.
