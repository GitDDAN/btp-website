John Newton's Letters

From the Service of Sin to Liberty (1892)

About These Letters

John Newton's autobiography, written in the form of letters to a friend, chronicles his remarkable journey from slave trader to beloved hymn writer and Anglican clergyman. Best known for writing "Amazing Grace," Newton's letters reveal the transforming power of God's grace in a life once given over to sin.

These fourteen letters cover his life from 1725 to 1757, when he was preparing for the ministry. They provide a powerful testimony of divine providence and redemption.

"During the time I was engaged in the slave trade I never had the least scruple as to its lawfulness... However, I considered myself as a sort of jailer or turnkey, and I was sometimes shocked with an employment that was perpetually conversant with chains, bolts, and shackles."

John Newton

1725 - 1807

English Anglican clergyman and abolitionist who wrote the hymn "Amazing Grace." His life transformation from slave trader to minister remains one of the most powerful testimonies of God's redeeming grace.

"Amazing grace! How sweet the sound that saved a wretch like me!"
