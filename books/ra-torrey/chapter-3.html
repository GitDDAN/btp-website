Chapter 3: Obeying and Praying

In 1 John 3:22 we read, "And whatsoever we ask, we receive of Him, because we keep His commandments, and do those things that are pleasing in His sight."

Here we find that which is perhaps the most important and vital lesson in connection with prayer. If we wish to pray with power, we must be obedient; we must do those things that are pleasing in God's sight.

Here is one of the reasons why so many prayers are not answered: the ones who offer them are not living in obedience to God's commandments. They are not doing what is pleasing to God. God makes no unconditional promises to the disobedient and rebellious. In Proverbs 28:9 we read, "He that turneth away his ear from hearing the law, even his prayer shall be abomination."

There is no use in praying unless we ourselves are willing to obey God. One who is living in any known sin has no right to expect that God will answer his prayers. If I regard iniquity in my heart, if I am cherishing some known sin in my thought or life, I have no right to expect that God will give heed to my prayers.

"If I regard iniquity in my heart, the Lord will not hear me." (Psalm 66:18)

Obedience in All Things

If we hold on to some sin, we thereby shut the ear of God against our cry. Many a prayer of fathers and mothers for their children remains unanswered because the fathers and mothers are not living right. They may be very anxious for the conversion of their children, but they have no right to expect it unless they themselves are walking before God in obedience. How can we have boldness to come to God about our children's salvation when we ourselves are not living according to His Word?

If we wish to have power in prayer, we must be earnest students of His Word to find out what His will is regarding ourselves, and then do it. One unconfessed act of disobedience on our part will shut the ear of God against many petitions.

Doing What Is Pleasing to Him

But it is not enough to keep God's commandments. We must do those things which are pleasing in His sight. This is going beyond mere obedience to the letter of the law. It means a life devoted to studying and doing God's will - not merely refraining from that which is forbidden, but actively pursuing what delights Him.

There are many things that are forbidden by no specific commandment of Scripture, but we easily discover that they are not pleasing to God. The Holy Spirit shows us what is and what is not pleasing to God. If we study to please Him in all that we do, then He will be gracious to us and give us whatever we ask.

The one who is not seeking to please God in all things has no right to expect God to answer his prayers. But the one who is living to please God can have what he will of God, if only he asks.

"Delight thyself also in the Lord: and He shall give thee the desires of thine heart." (Psalm 37:4)

Let us then search our lives thoroughly, put away all that is displeasing to God, and make it the ruling purpose of our lives to do what pleases Him. Then we shall have power in prayer, and we shall know what it means to have our prayers answered.
