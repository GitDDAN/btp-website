Historical Significance

Commissioned by King James I of England in 1604, the King James Version was completed in 1611 by a team of 47 scholars. It has profoundly influenced English literature, language, and culture for over four centuries.

Translation Philosophy

The KJV translators employed a formal equivalence approach, seeking to maintain the precise meaning and structure of the original Hebrew and Greek texts while producing dignified, memorable English prose.

Enduring Legacy

Many phrases from the KJV have become common expressions: "salt of the earth," "the powers that be," "fly in the ointment," and countless others that have shaped the English language.

Why We Use It

The Bible Teaching Program uses the KJV for its accuracy, literary beauty, and long tradition of faithful service to the Church. Its time-tested language continues to inspire devotion and careful study.
