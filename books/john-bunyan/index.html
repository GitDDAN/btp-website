About John Bunyan

John Bunyan was an English Puritan writer and preacher, best known for his Christian allegory The Pilgrim's Progress (1678). Written while Bunyan was imprisoned for preaching without a license, this masterwork has been translated into more than 200 languages and has never been out of print since its original publication.

Bunyan's powerful conversion testimony and his vivid allegorical storytelling have blessed countless believers through the centuries, making his works essential reading for understanding the Christian journey.
