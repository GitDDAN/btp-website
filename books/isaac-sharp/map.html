About These Maps

The Faroe Islands are an archipelago of 18 volcanic islands located between Norway and Iceland in the North Atlantic Ocean. These historical maps show the islands Isaac Sharp visited during his 1862 missionary journey, including Bordoy, Sandoy, Suderoy, and the capital Torshavn.

Historical Context

In 1862, the Faroe Islands were a remote Danish territory with a population of about 9,000. The islanders lived primarily by fishing and sheep farming. Isaac Sharp and Asbjorn Kloster traveled to these isolated communities to share the Gospel, often enduring harsh weather and difficult sea voyages between the islands.

The maps above show the same islands Sharp visited, though much has changed in the 160+ years since his journey.
