About This Work

Historical Context

This 1543 treatise represents Luther's late-life writings and reflects views that are now widely rejected. It is provided for historical and academic study of the Reformation era.

This work, written three years before Luther's death, is one of the more controversial writings of the Protestant Reformer. Scholars study it to understand the theological and social context of 16th-century Europe.

Historical Note

This work is preserved for historical and academic study. Many Lutheran churches and scholars have since rejected Luther's views expressed in this treatise. It is presented here as part of a comprehensive collection of Reformation-era documents.
