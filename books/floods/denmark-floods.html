FISHERFOLKFISHERFOLK

Fisherfolk have always been a unique breed of people. Fisherfolk har altid været en enestående race af mennesker. Living constantly in the shadow of a tragedy, a wife or a mother as she bids farewell to her loved ones leaving for a fishing trip has the secret fear that she may never see them again. Leve konstant i skyggen af en tragedie, en kone eller en mor, da hun bud farvel til sin elskede dem, der rejser til en fangstrejse har den hemmelige frygt for, at hun kan aldrig se dem igen. The fatality rate is very high in comparison with other industries. Har dødeligheden er meget høj sammenlignet med andre industrier.

Around the beginning of the century, the fishermen usually lived in parts of their towns near the seafront. Rundt om i begyndelsen af århundredet, fiskerne normalt boet i dele af deres byer nær seafront. Houses were made of granite and often had wooden floors. Huse blev lavet af granit og ofte havde træ gulve. In the living room would be the range-fire with a stool extending across the whole front of the range. I stuen vil være det interval-brand med en taburet strækker sig på tværs af hele forsiden af intervallet. Built into one wall would be the "Box-bed", which was a bed with three sides covered in, hence the name. Indbygget i en væg ville være "Box-seng", hvilket var en seng med tre sider er dækket ind, deraf navnet. This arrangement made the resting place warm and comfortable. Denne ordning gjorde hvilested varm og behagelig. At the rear of the dwelling was the part which contained the wash-tub and wringer. På bagsiden af boligen var den del, der indeholdt vaske-karbad og wringer. Nets were often mended in the garret upstairs or in some cases in an outhouse. Redskaber var ofte anbefales i kvist ovenpå eller i nogle tilfælde i en outhouse. In fine days during the summer, mending would be done outside. I fine dage i løbet af sommeren, lapning ville ske udenfor.

Some of the fishermen used lines to catch fish. Nogle af de fiskere, der anvendes linjer til at fange fisk. These lines were baited with mussels which were gathered from the seashore at a place known as the scaup. Disse linjer var agn med muslinger, som blev indsamlet fra kysten på et sted kaldet Scaup. This was mainly the task of the females, while the male members went to sea. Det var hovedsageligt en opgave af de hunner, mens de mandlige medlemmer gik til søs.   Whenever they reached a suitable age, the females would learn to mend nets, go to the scaup for mussels, to sheel and bait the lines. Når de var nået frem til en passende alder, hunnerne ville lære at reparere garn, skal du gå til Scaup for muslinger, plader og agn linjerne.

The staple diet of fishermen was plain foods, consisting of porridge and soups, with vegetable broth a speciality. De stapel kost fiskere blev plain fødevarer, der består af grød og supper, med vegetabilsk bouillon en specialitet. Saturday dinner was salt herring and tattie time. Lørdag middag var salt sild og tattie tid. The herring would be cured in a small barrel at the summer season with another barrel cured at the East Anglian fishing. De sild ville blive helbredt i en lille tønde på sommersæsonen med en anden tønde helbredt ved East Anglian fiskeri. Also on their menu would be dried fish, either ling or tusk. Også på deres menu ville være tørret fisk, enten lange eller brosme. These fish would be cleaned, split down the front, salted and dried on the rocks. Disse fisk vil blive renset, opdelt ned foran, saltet og tørret på klipperne.

In some cases church attendance was regular, each family going to their respective church. I nogle tilfælde kirke deltagerlisten var regelmæssig, hver familie at gå til deres respektive kirke. Every household had its own seat in the place of worship and parents would really feel proud going to church as a whole family. Hver husstand havde sin egen plads i stedet for tilbedelse og forældre vil virkelig føle sig stolte over at gå i kirke som en hel familie. For church the men would wear dark navy suits, while the women wore dresses down to the calves of their legs. For kirken mændene ville bære mørkt flåde dragter, mens kvinderne havde kjoler ned til kalve af deres ben. After the service, especially during the summer, the family would go for a short walk, but whenever they arrived home their Sunday clothes were exchanged for more casual dress. Efter den service, især om sommeren, familien ville gå i en kort gåtur, men når de kom hjem deres søn tøj blev udvekslet for mere afslappet påklædning. The Sunday clothes were also the attire worn at funerals, which were occasions of great mourning. The Sunday tøj var også dragt slidte på begravelser, som blev lejligheder af stor sorg.   When a disaster took place at sea, it would be a time of deep sorrow, whole communities lamented the loss of an individual or an entire crew. Når en katastrofe fandt sted på havet, ville det være en tid med dyb sorg, hele samfund beklaget tabet af en enkeltperson eller en hel besætning.

Weddings amongst fisherfolk were times of rejoicing. Bryllupper blandt fisherfolk var en tid med glæde. In the earlier part of this century, a young fisherman usually chose for his bride a lass from a 'fisher' family, knowing that she would have a first-hand idea of the hardships he faced. I den tidligere del af dette århundrede, en ung fisker normalt valgt for hans forlovede en pige fra en "fisker" familie, vel vidende, at hun ville have et første-hånds indtryk af de strabadser, han stod overfor. Another reason being that she would have learned to mend nets, a great asset to any fisherman who owned gear. En anden grund er, at hun ville have lært at reparere net, et stort aktiv for enhver fisker, der ejes gear.

A herring drifter crew consisted of 9 or 10 men including skipper-mate, five deckhands, cook and two engineers known as the "black squad". En sild drifter besætningen bestod af 9 eller 10 kvinder, herunder skipper-Mate, fem deckhands, kok og to ingeniører kendt som den "sorte hold".   Life on deck was very hard. Livet på dækket var meget hårdt. Seventy or eighty nets had to be pulled in all kinds of weather. Seventy eller firs redskaber skulle trækkes i alle slags vejr. It would be very difficult to get the nets a board if the wind changed direction after the nets were laid. Det ville være meget vanskeligt at få de redskaber en bestyrelse, hvis vinden ændrede retning efter de garn blev lagt. When the nets were on board, if the catch was reasonable, the nets had to be "red up" that is cleaned. Når garnene var om bord, hvis fangsten er rimelige, garnene skulle være "rød", der er rengjort. This was a task that was performed with extreme caution, as it was undertaken when the drifter was steaming at top speed towards port in order to obtain the highest price for the fish. Det var en opgave, som blev udført med ekstrem forsigtighed, da det blev iværksat, når drifter var dampning øverst hastighed i retning af havnen med henblik på at opnå den højeste pris for fisk. Since the deck crew were paid on a share basis, every minute counted in getting the catch ready for sale. Siden brobesætningen blev betalt på et share basis, hvert minut tælles med at få fangsten klar til salg. In charge of the engine room was the "driver" who kept the steam engine going full speed. Med ansvar for maskinrum var "fører" der holdes dampmaskinen igangværende fuld hastighed. The fireman was kept very busy stoking the boiler with coal. Den brandmand blev holdt meget travlt anspores til kedlen med kul. During hauling operations, if the catch was good, the engineer could "scum" the herring that dropped out of the nets. In doing so he earned what was known as "stoker", which was shared between himself, the fireman and the cook. I den forbindelse så han fortjent det, der blev kendt som "fyrbøder", som blev delt mellem ham selv, brandmand og kokken. The fireman also took his turn out of the engine room during the time the boat was fishing. Den brandmand tog også hans tur ud af maskinrummet under den tid båden var fiskeri. His task was to coil the leader rope on to which the nets were tied. Hans opgave var at coil lederen reb på hvilke redskaber blev bundet. This was a very monotonous job, lasting hours on end and it took place in a small locker room in the fore part of the vessel. Det var et meget ensformigt job, varig timer om afslutning, og den fandt sted i en lille locker room i forgrunden en del af fartøjet.   Cooking for nine or ten men was no easy task. Madlavning i ni eller ti mænd var ingen let opgave. As the nets were hauled in the cook's job was to pull in the "sol-rope" which was the lower part of the net. Som garnene blev trukket i kokken's opgave var at trække i "sol-reb", som var den nedre del af redskabet. After the nets were aboard the crew had to be fed. Madlavning blev gjort i den galej på et kulfyret komfur og rummet var begrænset. Three of the crew members were paid a weekly wage. Tre af de besætningsmedlemmer blev betalt en ugentlig løn. The engineer received around £1 10s (£1.50), the fireman around £1 5s (£1.25) and the same for the cook. Den ingeniør modtog omkring £ 1 10'erne (£ 1.50), brandmand omkring £ 1 5s (£ 1.25) og det samme for kokken. On top of this was the sum received for the herring mentioned before which provided these three men with "stoker". På toppen af dette var det beløb, som modtages for sild nævnt før, som har leveret disse tre mænd med "fyrbøder".

The 1914-18 war had just finished and men were coming home to their ordinary way of life. De 1914-18 krigen netop var færdige og mænd blev der kommer hjem til deres almindelige livsstil. Fishermen were returning from the Royal Naval Reserve Patrol to take up their work which had been interrupted by the call to arms. Fiskerne var vendt tilbage fra Royal Naval Reserve Patrol til at tage deres arbejde, der var blevet afbrudt af opfordringen til våben.

Steam drifters were replacing the sailing boats, some of which had been fitted with engines. Steam drifters blev erstatning af sejlbåd, hvoraf nogle var blevet udstyret med motorer. Nogle kuller linje bådene arbejdede lokalt i løbet af vinteren med et par større bådene komme til den "store linjer" fiskeri efter torsk, lange, brosme, rokke og hellefisk. Often the fishing season was a failure due to bad markets and lack of herring. Ofte fiskesæsonen var en fiasko på grund af dårlige markeder og manglen på sild.

It was among the fisher-folk living and working in such conditions and circumstances that God moved in power during the later months of 1921. Det var blandt de Fisher-folk lever og arbejder i sådanne forhold og omstændigheder, at Gud flyttes ved magten i løbet af de senere måneder af 1921.
