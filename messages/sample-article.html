Walking by Faith

2 Corinthians 5:7

A reflection on what it means to trust God when we cannot see the path ahead.

Steve Dulwich • 5 min read

Key Scripture

"For we walk by faith, not by sight."

— 2 Corinthians 5:7 (KJV)

The Christian life is fundamentally a walk of faith. From the moment we first believe until the day we see Christ face to face, we are called to trust in what we cannot see, to hope for what has not yet been realized, and to rest in promises that seem impossible by human standards.

What Does It Mean to Walk by Faith?

To "walk by faith" means to live our daily lives guided not by what our physical eyes can see, but by what God has revealed in His Word. It means making decisions based on God's promises rather than our circumstances. It means trusting His character when we cannot trace His hand.

"Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen."
— Hebrews 11:1

Abraham is our great example of this. When God called him to leave his homeland and go to a place he had never seen, Abraham obeyed. He didn't have a map. He didn't have GPS coordinates. He simply had a promise from God—and that was enough.

The Challenge of Our Age

We live in an age that demands proof. We want data, statistics, and guarantees before we make any significant decision. But God often asks us to step out before He shows us the way. He asks us to trust before we understand.

This is not blind faith—it is faith grounded in the character of God, who has proven Himself faithful throughout history and in our own lives. We walk by faith, but it is faith in a trustworthy God.

Three Practical Steps

  • Immerse yourself in Scripture — Faith comes by hearing, and hearing by the Word of God (Romans 10:17). The more we know God's Word, the more we can trust His promises.
  • Remember His faithfulness — Keep a record of answered prayers and moments when God came through. These become testimonies that strengthen our faith for future challenges.
  • Take the next step — Faith without works is dead. Sometimes we need to simply take the next step God is showing us, even when we can't see the whole staircase.

A Final Word

The walk of faith is not always easy. There will be moments of doubt, seasons of waiting, and times when God seems silent. But remember: the same God who parted the Red Sea, who raised Jesus from the dead, who has sustained His Church for two thousand years—He is walking with you.

You are not alone on this journey. And one day, faith will become sight. Until then, we walk by faith—one step at a time, trusting in the One who holds our future.

"Trust in the LORD with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding."

— Proverbs 3:5
