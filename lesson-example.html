Lesson 1 Foundation Course

The Word of God

Understanding the authority and reliability of Scripture

Introduction

The Bible is unlike any other book. It is God's written revelation to humanity, containing everything we need to know about Him, His plan for our lives, and how we can have a relationship with Him through Jesus Christ.

The Authority of Scripture

When we say the Bible is authoritative, we mean that it carries the weight of God's own words. All Scripture is God-breathed (2 Timothy 3:16), which means that God Himself inspired the human authors to write exactly what He wanted them to write.

"All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the servant of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work." - 2 Timothy 3:16-17

Key Points About Biblical Authority

  • Divine Inspiration: The Bible comes from God, not merely human ideas
  • Complete Truth: Everything the Bible teaches is true and reliable
  • Final Authority: The Bible is the ultimate standard for faith and practice
  • Sufficient Guide: Scripture contains everything we need for salvation and godly living

The Reliability of Scripture

The Bible has been tested throughout history and has proven itself reliable time and again. Archaeological discoveries continue to confirm biblical accounts, and the prophecies found in Scripture have been fulfilled with remarkable accuracy.

Evidence of Reliability

  1. Manuscript Evidence: We have more ancient manuscripts of the Bible than any other ancient document
  2. Archaeological Confirmation: Countless archaeological finds support biblical accounts
  3. Fulfilled Prophecy: Hundreds of specific prophecies have been precisely fulfilled
  4. Internal Consistency: Despite being written over 1,500 years by 40+ authors, the Bible maintains a unified message

Practical Application

Because the Bible is God's authoritative and reliable Word, we should:

  • Read it regularly to know God better
  • Study it carefully to understand His will
  • Obey its teachings in our daily lives
  • Share its message with others

Conclusion

The Word of God is our foundation for faith and life. As we build our lives on Scripture, we can be confident that we are building on solid rock that will never fail us.
